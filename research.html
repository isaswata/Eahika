Knowledge & Inquiry

Research

Rigorous, interdisciplinary, and community-engaged research across twelve critical areas of human development, social inquiry, and environmental challenge.

What We Study

Research Areas

Our research spans twelve interconnected domains. We embrace cross-disciplinary inquiry, recognizing that the most important questions refuse to stay inside a single box.

🏥

Public Health

Community health systems, epidemiology, health equity, and the social determinants of health — especially in under-resourced and marginalized contexts.

🌍

Climate Change & Related Issues

Climate science, environmental justice, mitigation and adaptation strategies, and the social, economic, and political dimensions of the climate crisis.

⚠️

Disaster Risk Reduction & Adaptation

Disaster governance, community resilience, early warning systems, and integrated approaches to disaster risk reduction and climate adaptation.

♻️

Sustainable Development

SDG frameworks, circular economies, just transitions, and the integration of sustainability principles into policy and practice at local and global scales.

🏙️

Urban Space, Life & Livelihood

Urbanization, informal settlements, housing rights, urban ecology, public space, and the livelihood strategies of urban communities.

🏛️

Public Policy & Governance

Policy analysis, democratic governance, decentralization, accountability mechanisms, and the politics of public institutions.

📚

Critical Theory & Knowledge System

Decolonial epistemologies, knowledge politics, critical theory traditions, and the social production of knowledge itself.

🎓

Society & Education

Education systems, educational inequalities, pedagogy, and the relationship between schooling, society, and social transformation.

🔬

Science, Technology & Society

STS perspectives on technology governance, digital equity, AI ethics, and the co-production of scientific knowledge and social order.

🤝

Community Practices

Participatory action research, community-based approaches, indigenous knowledge systems, and grassroots organizing.

⚖️

Social Inequalities

Caste, class, gender, race, disability — the structures and lived experiences of inequality, and movements working toward justice.

👷

Labor Studies

Work, workers, and workplaces — labor rights, informal economies, gig work, trade unions, and the changing face of labor in an era of globalization.
